Workout 4

Description

  • Starting Position:
    • Stand up straight with your feet shoulder-width apart.
    • Keep your chest up, shoulders back, and your gaze straight ahead or slightly upward.
  • Engage Your Core:
    • Tighten your core muscles to maintain stability and support your spine.
  • Initiate the Squat:
    • Begin the squat by pushing your hips back as if you're sitting into a chair.
    • Imagine lowering your body down and back, not just bending your knees.
    • As you lower your body, keep your chest up, and your back straight (avoid rounding your back).
  • Bend Your Knees:
    • As you continue to lower yourself, bend your knees.
    • Aim to lower your hips until your thighs are parallel to the ground or as far down as your mobility and strength allow.
    • Ensure your knees stay aligned with your feet and do not collapse inward.
  • Hold the Squat Position:
    • At the bottom of the squat, your weight should be on your heels, and your knees should be above your ankles.
    • Your back should remain straight, and your chest should still be up.
  • Return to the Starting Position:
    • Push through your heels and extend your hips and knees simultaneously to return to the starting position.
    • Exhale as you stand up.
  • Repeat:
    • Perform the desired number of repetitions. Squats are often done in sets of 8-12 reps.

Sets:3

Rep:10

Additional Details

  • Warm up before performing squats to prevent injury.
  • Pay attention to your breathing; exhale as you push up and inhale as you lower down.
  • Use a mirror or have someone watch your form to ensure you're performing squats correctly.
  • As you become more experienced, you can add weights, like dumbbells or a barbell, to increase resistance.
