Workout 1:PushUps

Description

  • Starting Position:
    • Begin in a plank position with your hands placed slightly wider than shoulder-width apart.
    • Your body should form a straight line from head to heels, and keep your feet together.
  • Hand Placement:
    • Position your hands firmly on the ground with your fingers pointing forward or slightly turned outward.
    • Ensure that your palms are under your shoulders.
  • Engage Core:
    • Tighten your core muscles to maintain a straight body.
    • Avoid arching your lower back or letting your hips sag.
  • Lowering Phase:
    • Bend your elbows and lower your body toward the ground.
    • Keep your elbows close to your body, not flaring out to the sides.
    • Lower your chest until it nearly touches the ground, or as far down as your strength allows while maintaining good form.
  • Pushing Phase:
    • Push through your palms and straighten your arms to lift your body back to the starting position.
    • Fully extend your arms without locking your elbows.
  • Repeat:
    • Perform the desired number of repetitions.
    • Beginners may start with a lower number and gradually increase over time.

Sets: 3

Reps: 10

Additional Details

  • Maintain proper form throughout the exercise to avoid strain or injury.
  • Keep your head in a neutral position, looking down at the ground, to align your spine.
  • Focus on controlled movements, and don't rush through the exercise.
  • It's okay to start with modified push-ups (e.g., from your knees) if full push-ups are too challenging initially.
  • Gradually increase the number of push-ups you can do as you build strength.
