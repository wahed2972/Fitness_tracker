Workout 6

Description

  • Starting Position:
    • Lie on your back on a comfortable, flat surface (e.g., yoga mat).
    • Bend your knees and place your feet flat on the floor, hip-width apart.
    • Cross your arms over your chest or place your hands gently behind your head.
  • Engage Your Core:
    • Tighten your core muscles by drawing your belly button toward your spine.
    • Create a slight arch in your lower back against the floor.
  • Crunch:
    • Exhale as you lift your upper body off the floor using your abdominal muscles.
    • Move your ribcage toward your pelvis while keeping your lower back on the ground.
    • Lift your head, neck, and shoulders together.
  • Hold the Contraction:
    • At the top of the movement, squeeze your abdominal muscles for a brief pause (the "crunched" position).
  • Lower Back Down:
    • Inhale as you slowly and controlledly lower your upper body back down to the starting position, one vertebra at a time.
    • Keep your lower back in contact with the floor throughout.
  • Repeat:
    • Perform the desired number of repetitions. Sets of 12-20 reps are common for crunches.

Sets:2

Rep:10

Additional Details

  • Focus on the quality of the movement: Concentrate on using your abdominal muscles rather than the quantity of reps.
  • Breathe properly: Exhale as you lift your upper body and inhale as you lower it down.
  • Keep your movements controlled: Avoid using momentum to lift your upper body; instead, use your core strength.
  • Variations: To challenge your core further, consider trying different crunch variations, such as bicycle crunches or reverse crunches.
  • Consult a fitness professional: If you're new to abdominal exercises or have any back issues, consider seeking guidance from a fitness expert to ensure you're performing the exercise safely.
