Workout 3

Description

  • Starting Position:
    • Stand up straight with your feet shoulder-width apart.
    • Hold a barbell with both hands using an underhand grip (palms facing upward), and your hands should be slightly wider than shoulder-width apart.
  • Grip and Position:
    • Let the barbell hang at arm's length in front of your thighs.
    • Keep your elbows close to your torso throughout the exercise.
  • Engage Core:
    • Tighten your core muscles to stabilize your spine and maintain a straight posture.
  • Curling Phase:
    • Exhale and slowly curl the barbell upward by bending your elbows.
    • Keep your upper arms stationary; only your forearms should move.
    • Continue curling until the barbell is at shoulder level or slightly below, and your biceps are fully contracted.
  • Squeeze and Hold:
    • At the top of the movement, squeeze your biceps for a brief pause to maximize muscle contraction.
  • Lowering Phase:
    • Inhale and slowly lower the barbell back to the starting position.
    • Fully extend your arms without locking your elbows.
  • Repeat:
    • Perform the desired number of repetitions.
    • Start with a weight that allows you to maintain proper form, and gradually increase the weight as you progress.

Set:3

Rep:10

Additional Details

  • Maintain proper form throughout the exercise to avoid strain or injury.
  • Avoid using your back or shoulders to lift the weight; focus on isolating your biceps.
  • Use a controlled and deliberate motion, avoiding any swinging or momentum.
  • Perform the exercise with a full range of motion, both during the curling and lowering phases.
  • You can use an EZ-curl bar if you have wrist discomfort with a straight barbell.
  • Variations like preacher curls or incline curls can target the biceps differently.
