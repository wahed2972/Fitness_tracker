Workout 2

Description

  • Grip the Bar:
    • Approach a pull-up bar or an overhead horizontal bar.
    • Stand facing the bar and reach up to grip it with your palms facing away from you (pronated grip). Your hands should be slightly wider than shoulder-width apart.
  • Hang From the Bar:
    • Hang from the bar with your arms fully extended.
    • Your body should be in a dead hang, and your feet should not touch the ground.
  • Engage Your Core:
    • Tighten your core muscles to maintain a straight body position. Avoid excessive swinging.
  • Initiate the Pull:
    • Begin the pull by squeezing your shoulder blades together.
    • Pull your chest towards the bar while keeping your elbows pointing down and back.
  • Chin Over the Bar:
    • Continue pulling until your chin is over the bar, and your upper chest almost touches it.
    • Your elbows should be fully bent at this point.
  • Lowering Phase:
    • Lower your body back down to the fully extended hanging position in a controlled manner.
    • Fully extend your arms without locking your elbows.
  • Repeat:
    • Perform the desired number of repetitions.
    • Beginners may start with a lower number and gradually increase over time.

Sets:3

Rep:5

Additional Details

  • Maintain proper form throughout the exercise to avoid strain or injury.
  • Use a full range of motion, going from a dead hang to chin over the bar.
  • Avoid using excessive momentum or swinging to complete the repetition.
  • You can vary the grip width and hand positions (e.g., wide grip, narrow grip, or palms facing you) to target different muscle groups.
  • If you're new to pull-ups, you can use assistance bands or a pull-up machine to make them more accessible.
  • As you gain strength, aim to increase the number of pull-ups you can do or try more challenging variations.
